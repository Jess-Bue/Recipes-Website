Dragon Rolls

Dragon rolls are tradtionally made with either tempura shrimp, unagi, or crab as the filling. While the top layer is made up of thinly sliced avacado.

Ingredients

  • Japanese or Persian Cucumber
  • 2 Avacados
  • 1/2 Lemon
  • 2 Sheets Nori
  • 2 Cups Sushi Rice (cooked and seasoned)
  • 8 Pieces Shrimp Tempura
  • 2 Tbsp Tobiko
  • Unagi Fillet

Toppings

  • Spicy Mayo
  • Unagi Sauce
  • Toasted Black Sesame Seeds

Vinegar Water

  • 1/4 Cup Water
  • 2 Tsp Rice Vinegar (unseasoned)

Instructions

  1. Cut cucumbers lenghtwise into quarters. Remove seeds and again cut in half lengthwise into thin strips
  2. Cut avacado in half and remove pit
  3. Remove skin and thinly slice avacado crosswise
  4. Gently press sliced avacado pieces flat, while slightly seperating, to the length of the nori
  5. Line the bamboo mat with plastic wrap and place half of the nori sheet on top, shiny side down
  6. Dip hands into vinegar water and spread 1/2 cup sushi rice onto nori sheet
  7. Flip over the nori sheet and place two pieces of shimp tempura and two cucumber strips. Add some tobiko towards the end of the sheet
  8. From the bottom end, start tightly rolling the nori sheet over the filling until the bottom edge reaches the end of nori sheet. Tightly roll over the remaning nori
  9. Remove the bamboo mat and plastic wrap from the roll. Use the side of the knife to lift the avacado slices onto the top of the roll
  10. Wrap the top of the roll with plastic wrap and gently squeeze until the avacado slices wrap around the sides of the roll
  11. Cut the roll through the plastic wrap into 8 pieces. Cleaning the knife between each cut. Once sliced, gently remove plastic wrap
  12. Cover each piece in tobiko and drizzle with spicy mayo and finish with a sprinkle of black sesame seeds
